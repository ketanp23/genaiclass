Why Prompt Engineering is a Business Skill
AI outputs are only as good as the instructions they receive. In this lab, you'll follow MedCore Health, Verdant Retail, and Nexus Capital — three organizations whose teams are learning to prompt with precision, structure, and strategic intent.

Unstructured (common)
Summarize this report.

You are MedCore's CFO. Summarize the Q3 hospital operations report in 4 bullets — revenue, cost overruns, staffing ratios, and one forward risk. Audience: Board of Directors. Max 120 words. Flag any metric that missed target by >10%.
Direction
Prompts tell the model what role to play, what task to perform, and what constraints to observe.
Precision
Vague inputs yield vague outputs. Structure produces consistent, high-quality results every time.
Efficiency
The right prompt eliminates back-and-forth, saving hours across an organization each week.
Scale
Prompt templates become reusable assets — shared, refined, and embedded in team workflows.

The CREATE Framework
CREATE is a six-part framework for complex, high-stakes prompts. Character · Request · Examples · Adjustments · Type · Extras. Each component removes ambiguity and gives the model everything it needs to produce expert-grade output.
⏱ 10 min
C · R · E · A · T · E — Every letter explained
C
Character / RoleWho is the AI? Persona, expertise, experience level, and communication style.
R
Request / TaskThe exact action needed. Use a verb + object + scope.
E
Examples / ContextBackground data, reference documents, or example outputs that calibrate the response.
A
Adjustments / ConstraintsGuardrails: word limits, excluded topics, regulatory boundaries, tone restrictions.
T
Type of OutputFormat specification: bullet list, table, memo, JSON, slide outline, email.
E
Extras / EvaluationMeta-instructions: confidence rating, flag assumptions, ask clarifying questions.

✏ Exercise — Build a CREATE Prompt
Choose your organization and write a full CREATE prompt for a challenge they face. Address all 6 components explicitly.
C: You are MedCore's Chief People Officer with 20 years of hospital workforce management experience.
R: Develop a nurse retention strategy for ICU units where turnover hit 28% in 2024 — 14pts above benchmark.
E: Exit data: burnout 41%, scheduling 33%, pay gap 26%. Competitor hired 60 ICU nurses away.
A: Actionable within 6 months. No options over $2M capital. No hiring freezes.
T: Prioritized action plan: 3 actions in 30 days, 3 in 90 days, 1 structural change at 180 days.
E: Rate confidence per recommendation. Flag policy assumptions.

The RTF Framework
RTF is the executive's speed formula: Role · Task · Format. Use it for everyday professional tasks where you need a quality output fast.
⏱ 7 min
R · T · F — The 60-second professional prompt
R
RoleAct as a [title] with expertise in [domain]. Sets the knowledge base and voice.
T
TaskDraft / Analyze / Create / Summarize [specific thing] given [context]. Be precise.
F
FormatReturn as [structure] with [sections/length]. Define what you want to read.
MedCore Health — RTF Examples Healthcare
Verdant Retail — RTF Examples Retail
Nexus Capital — RTF Examples Finance
RTF vs CREATE: Use RTF for routine, time-pressured tasks. Switch to CREATE when the task has multiple dimensions, high stakes, or needs precise calibration.
✏ Exercise — RTF in 90 Seconds
Verdant's Head of CX Nina Shah needs talking points for a press call about viral complaint thread on damaged eco-packaging. Write her RTF prompt.
R: Act as a senior PR strategist specializing in consumer brand crisis communications and sustainability narratives.

T: Prepare talking points for Nina Shah, Verdant's Head of CX, speaking to a journalist about a viral tweet claiming eco-packaging arrived damaged. Verdant's position: investigating, sustainability standards apply end-to-end, affected customers are being prioritized.

F: 5 talking points, each 2 sentences. Open with empathy, end with forward commitment. Avoid defensive language. Avoid "eco-friendly" — use "sustainable packaging standards."

Zero-Shot Prompting
Zero-shot prompts give no examples — the model relies entirely on its training. They work for well-defined, common tasks. The key is being specific about task, constraints, and format.
⏱ 7 min
MedCore Health — Zero-Shot Examples Healthcare
Verdant Retail — Zero-Shot Examples Retail
Nexus Capital — Zero-Shot Examples Finance
Weak zero-shot
Analyze this contract.
Strong zero-shot
You are a healthcare attorney specializing in vendor contracts. Review this MSA and list all clauses that create liability risk for MedCore. Format: numbered list sorted by severity (High/Med/Low). For each: cite the clause, name the risk type. [PASTE CONTRACT]
Zero-shot struggles with highly specialized formatting or niche domain calibration. When output is inconsistent — add few-shot examples.
✏ Exercise — Zero-Shot Prompt
Nexus Capital's Sophie Wang needs to draft a quarterly LP update email covering portfolio performance. Write a precise zero-shot prompt — no examples.
You are a seasoned investor relations professional at a PE firm managing $4.2B AUM. Draft a Q3 2024 LP update email on behalf of Sophie Wang at Nexus Capital.

Context: Blended MOIC 2.1x. 3 companies with EBITDA growth >15%. 2 underperforming. One exit at 3.4x. New acquisition closed in September.

Constraints: Professional, confident, transparent — not promotional. Acknowledge underperformers directly. No vague reassurances. Max 380 words.

Format: Subject line (2 options) + 4-paragraph email: (1) Overall health, (2) Highlights, (3) Challenges and actions, (4) Q4 outlook and LP call invite.

Few-Shot Prompting
Few-shot prompting provides 2–5 input-output examples before the actual task. Examples teach format, tone, reasoning style, and domain vocabulary — showing the model exactly what you expect.
⏱ 10 min
MedCore Health — Few-Shot Examples Healthcare
Verdant Retail — Few-Shot Examples Retail
Nexus Capital — Few-Shot Examples Finance
Pro tip: Two excellent, diverse examples outperform five mediocre ones. Choose examples that represent edge cases and variation — not just the easy, obvious cases.
✏ Exercise — Few-Shot Prompt
Verdant's Brand Director Lena Wolff wants Claude to write product descriptions in her voice — warm, direct, sustainability-focused, no buzzwords. Write a 2-shot prompt, then ask for a linen blazer description.
Write Verdant product descriptions matching my exact voice.

Example 1 — Organic cotton tee:
"Made from 100% GOTS-certified organic cotton. Soft enough to sleep in, structured enough for Mondays. No dyes you can't pronounce. A tee built to last 10 years, not 10 washes."

Example 2 — Recycled wool coat:
"This coat began as six wool sweaters. We unraveled them, re-spun the fiber, and wove something better. The weight of something that has already lived a life. Designed for another 20 years."

Rules: Short sentences. One tactile detail. One sourcing fact. No buzzwords (eco-friendly, sustainable, planet, green). Under 60 words.

Now write for: Unlined linen blazer, Belgian linen, no synthetic blends, wrinkle-resistant.

Chain-of-Thought Prompting
CoT prompts ask the model to show its reasoning step-by-step before giving a final answer. This dramatically improves accuracy on complex reasoning and strategic decisions.
⏱ 8 min
MedCore — CoT in action: ICU staffing crisis Healthcare
P
Prompt (Priya Varma, Director of Operations)
MedCore's North Campus ICU is 40% understaffed this weekend due to a flu outbreak. We have 18 critical patients. Think through this situation step by step before recommending an action plan.
1
Step 1 — Assess immediate risk
18 critical patients with 40% staffing shortfall means current nurse-to-patient ratios likely violate ICU safety standards (typically 1:2). Patient safety is the primary constraint.
2
Step 2 — Identify available resources
Options: (a) call-in nurses from South Campus ICU, (b) activate float pool, (c) request agency nurses (48hr lead time — too slow), (d) temporarily transfer stable patients to step-down units.
3
Step 3 — Evaluate constraints
South Campus transfer disrupts their coverage. Float pool: likely limited for ICU-certified nurses. Patient transfer requires physician approval and bed availability in step-down.
✓
Recommendation
Immediate: (1) Activate float pool for ICU-certified nurses. (2) Request physician review — transfer 4–6 most stable patients to step-down. (3) Place South Campus on standby. Escalate to CMO if float pool yields fewer than 4 nurses.
CoT examples across organizations All Domains
"Think step by step"
The classic CoT trigger. Simple, highly effective.
"Before answering, list your assumptions"
Forces explicit assumption identification — critical for ambiguous situations.
"Walk me through your reasoning"
Produces narrative-style chains — good for executive communication.
"Show your work, then conclude"
Separates analysis from recommendation — ideal when reasoning IS the value.
✏ Exercise — Chain-of-Thought
Nexus Capital's Managing Partner Elena Vasquez must decide whether to inject $8M more into a struggling portfolio company (B2B logistics SaaS with 60% revenue from one customer). Write a CoT prompt that makes Claude reason through this systematically.
You are a senior PE operating partner. Before giving any recommendation, think through this decision step by step.

Step 1: Quantify the 60% customer concentration risk — valuation discount, churn downside.
Step 2: Evaluate what $8M achieves in 12–18 months for this company.
Step 3: Examine the no-injection scenario — liquidation value, covenant risk.
Step 4: Assess management — execution problem or structural market problem?
Step 5: Model exit scenarios at current vs. post-injection trajectory. Fund II is in year 6 of 10.

Context: $6M ARR, 60% from Walmart, NRR 84%, EBITDA -$400K. Nexus invested $14M 18 months ago. FMV ~$10M.

Only after completing all steps: Invest / Partial / Do Not Invest + top 3 reasons + the single assumption that reverses your answer.

Detailed Long-Form Prompts
For mission-critical work — board decks, strategic plans, legal reviews, M&A memos — long-form prompts combine frameworks, rich context, explicit constraints, and output schemas.
⏱ 8 min
MedCore — Full long-form prompt: Digital transformation roadmap Healthcare
# ROLE CHARACTER: You are a Principal-level healthcare IT consultant, ex-Deloitte Digital Health practice. You have led EHR migrations for 8 hospital systems and advised on HIPAA-compliant cloud architecture. # TASK REQUEST: Create a 3-year digital transformation roadmap for MedCore Health transitioning from legacy on-premise EHR (Epic v2018) to a cloud-native, interoperable care platform. # CONTEXT CONTEXT: - 3,200 staff. $480M revenue. 6 hospitals, 40 clinics. - Current: Epic on-premise + 14 point solutions (siloed). - 22% of clinical staff report documentation taking >3 hrs/shift. - Goal: Reduce documentation burden by 40%, improve care coordination. - Budget: $22M over 3 years. 12 internal IT engineers available. # CONSTRAINTS ADJUSTMENTS: - Must maintain HIPAA and ONC compliance throughout. - Cannot have EHR downtime >2 hours during any migration window. - Prioritize clinician experience over administrative efficiency. - All vendors must have SOC 2 Type II certification. # OUTPUT TYPE: Section 1: Executive summary (150 words) Section 2: Current state gap analysis (table) Section 3: 3-phase roadmap — Year 1 / Year 2 / Year 3 Section 4: Governance and ownership model Section 5: KPIs — baseline, Year 1 target, Year 3 target # META EXTRAS: - Flag every assumption with [ASSUMPTION] - If recommending a technology category, name 2 specific vendors - Rate overall risk: Low / Medium / High with justification - End with 3 questions you'd ask MedCore's CIO before finalizing
