The LLM model landscape

LLMs are not interchangeable. Every model sits on a spectrum of capability, speed, and cost — and the right choice depends entirely on your task. Understanding this landscape is the prerequisite to every AI deployment decision.

The five selection dimensions

Every model selection decision can be decomposed into five orthogonal dimensions. Executives who understand these dimensions can set clear procurement criteria and evaluate vendor claims objectively.

Priority ranking from your weights

    What each dimension means for model selection

    DimensionLow importance → choiceHigh importance → choice
    Task complexityFast/lightweight model sufficientFrontier model required
    LatencyAny tier acceptableFast model mandatory; frontier too slow
    Cost sensitivityFrontier acceptableFast/mid-tier; aggressive routing strategy
    Privacy / sovereigntyCloud API fineOn-prem or private cloud deployment
    Output reliabilitySingle model call acceptableEnsemble, validation layer, or human-in-loop

    Common mistake: Most organizations default to frontier models for everything, treating it as the "safe" choice. It is not — it is the expensive choice. A model that is 10× the cost but only 15% better on a simple task is a poor capital allocation decision, regardless of how impressive the benchmark score looks.
