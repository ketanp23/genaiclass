§ 01 · The evaluation framework

The twelve parameters that decide whether your RAG is working.

RAG evaluation isn't one metric. It's a stack of measurements arranged in two layers: the retrieval layer (did we fetch the right stuff?) and the generation layer (did we answer well from it?). For graph and agentic RAG, two more layers stack on top: traversal quality and agent behaviour. Skip any layer and a failure mode hides behind it.

Layer 1 · Retrieval quality — Did we fetch the right things?

RETRIEVAL
01Context Precision

Of the chunks we retrieved, what fraction were actually relevant to the question? High precision means we're not drowning the LLM in noise.

If we retrieved 10 chunks and 7 actually relate to the question, precision = 0.70.

RETRIEVAL
02Context Recall

Of all the relevant information that exists in the corpus, what fraction did we retrieve? Recall protects against quietly missing a clause that mattered.

Did we miss any clause the regulator would want to see cited?

RETRIEVAL
03Context Relevance

For each retrieved chunk, how on-topic is it? An LLM-judge or human rater scores chunks against the query. Cheap to compute, surprisingly diagnostic.

Are the top-5 chunks all on-topic, or is rank #3 a near-miss from a different policy?

RETRIEVAL
04MRR / Hit Rate @ K

Where does the first correct chunk appear in the ranked results? Mean Reciprocal Rank rewards getting the right answer to the top of the list.

Is the right passage at rank #1, or does the model have to wade through 8 wrong ones to find it?

Layer 2 · Generation quality — Did we answer well from the retrieval?

GENERATION
05Faithfulness

Is every claim in the answer supported by the retrieved context? This is the anti-hallucination metric. Each generated sentence is checked against the sources.

Can every sentence in the answer be traced back to a retrieved passage?

GENERATION
06Answer Relevance

Did the answer actually address what was asked? A faithful answer can still be off-topic. Relevance catches non-sequiturs and over-broad responses.

If you asked about refund timeframes, does the answer address timeframes — or wander into refund policy in general?

GENERATION
07Answer Correctness

Compared to a ground-truth answer (curated by a human SME), is the generated answer factually right? Requires labelled test sets but is the gold standard.

Does this match what an expert would have written?

GENERATION
08Citation Coverage

What fraction of the answer's claims cite a verifiable source? For regulated industries this is non-negotiable. An uncited claim is, formally, hallucinated.

Could an auditor open every source mentioned and verify the claim it underpins?

Layer 3 · Graph & agent extensions — When the pattern is more than retrieve-and-answer.

GRAPH RAG
09Path Validity

For each multi-hop answer, is the graph path the system traversed actually correct — every edge real, every entity properly resolved?

Does the chain "Acme → Cayman → Petrov" reflect actual ownership edges in the graph, with sources for each?

GRAPH RAG
10Subgraph Completeness

Did the traversal return all paths that matter, not just one? Missing an alternative chain is as bad as inventing one.

Are there other 4-hop chains from the customer to a sanctioned entity we didn't surface?

AGENTIC RAG
11Tool-Call Accuracy

For each sub-task, did the agent pick the right tool and call it with sensible arguments? A wrong tool choice cascades into the rest of the run.

Should the agent have hit the CRM or the news API? Did it choose well?

AGENTIC RAG
12Trajectory Efficiency

How many tool calls did the agent need? Did it loop usefully or wander? Efficiency = quality-of-answer ÷ number-of-steps.

Did the agent reach the right answer in 5 tool calls or did it stumble through 17?

02
Standard RAG · Retail · Store operations

Store Manager's Finance Co-Pilot

TEXT-TO-SQL
STRUCTURED

The setting. A national apparel retailer's 320 store managers were drowning the central analytics team with ad-hoc questions: "Why did my margin drop last week?" "What's selling badly?" "How does my markdown rate compare to my district?" The data lives in a Snowflake warehouse with 40+ tables — sales, inventory, markdowns, payroll. A structured RAG sits in front of the warehouse: manager asks in plain English, the system writes and runs SQL, the LLM explains the numbers. One question. One query. One numeric answer, explained.

What the metrics tell us

Structured RAG moves the failure mode upstream: numbers can't be hallucinated because they come from the warehouse, so faithfulness on figures is essentially free. The risk is in writing the wrong query. Investment shifts to schema documentation, a business glossary, and few-shot examples — the LLM needs to know that "margin" means (revenue − cogs)/revenue, not gross_margin_field.

Graph RAG · Legal · Contract intelligence

Contract Obligation Networks

MULTI-HOP
RELATIONAL

The setting. A global engineering group has 14,000 active contracts across 38 subsidiaries. A regulatory change forces a question: "Which of our contracts contain a most-favoured-nation clause that, if triggered by our pending deal with Customer X, would automatically force price changes in upstream supplier agreements?" What the metrics tell us

Graph RAG swaps the failure mode: precision and faithfulness become near-trivially high, because the structure of the graph constrains answers. The real risk migrates to completeness — did the graph contain the edge we needed? Here, a subsidiary linkage was missing, so the system missed Supplier C entirely. The investment must shift from retrieval tuning to graph hygiene: entity resolution, edge extraction quality, and coverage audits.

Agentic RAG · Consulting · Research synthesis

Market Entry Research Agent

MULTI-TOOL
ITERATIVE

The setting. A strategy consulting firm's senior partner asks: "Should our client enter the Brazilian EV-charging market in 2026? Give me the demand outlook, the competitive landscape, the regulatory situation, and the top three risks — with sources." No single retrieval answers this. The agent must plan, route, retrieve, evaluate, loop, and synthesise — across the firm's research library, public filings, news, and external market databases.

What the metrics tell us

Agentic RAG adds two new axes — tool choice and trajectory length — that standard metrics can't see. Faithfulness and citation stay strong because the loop forces sourcing. The watch-list moves to efficiency: an agent that takes 17 tool calls to answer a 4-call question is expensive, slow, and signals a planning bug. Cost-per-query becomes a first-class metric, alongside quality.

§ 02 · The decision framework

When to reach for which.

The patterns are not a hierarchy — agentic isn't "better than" standard. Each is the right answer to a different shape of question. The table below is the practitioner's first cut: nine dimensions, four patterns, one row per decision you need to make before kicking off a project.

Decide in four lines.

Pick RAG · Unstructured

IF the answer lives in a passage
AND one retrieval is enough
AND you need it in under 2 seconds

Pick RAG · Structured

IF the answer is a number
AND the data is in a warehouse
AND figures must be exact, not estimated

Pick Graph RAG

IF the answer is a path
AND relationships matter more than prose
AND auditors will ask for the trail

Pick Agentic RAG

IF the answer requires deciding
AND sources are many and mixed
AND minutes-not-seconds is acceptable
