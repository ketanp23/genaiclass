In two hours, every executive in the room will leave with a working web prototype of an internal tool they actually need — built entirely through conversation with AI. No engineers. No mock-ups. / 01

Why vibecoding belongs on an executive's desk.

The Shift

For a generation, "I have an idea for a tool" meant a six-month engineering queue. Today, the same sentence — typed into the right AI — produces a working prototype before the meeting ends. The bottleneck has moved from building to articulating.

Why Executives

The leaders who can describe a problem precisely, scope it ruthlessly, and judge a prototype critically are now the most productive builders in their company. This workshop hands them the muscle memory — not the syntax — to do that.

What Vibecoding Means

Building software by stating intent in natural language and steering an AI through iteration. The deliverable is the same as code; the medium is conversation. Quality comes from clarity of thought, not knowledge of frameworks.

What You Walk Out With

A deployed, shareable URL of a working internal tool. A reusable PRD template. A library of prompts. And — the real point — the conviction that the next problem on your desk is something you can prototype yourself, this week.

/ 02

The build: ClientPulse.

internal · b2b · ops
ClientPulse
Account health, at a glance, before it's a crisis.

The scenario. You lead a 40-person consulting firm with 32 active client accounts. Right now, the "state of each account" lives in spreadsheets, Slack threads, and the heads of individual partners. Renewal conversations get ambushed by problems that were visible weeks earlier — to someone, somewhere.

The ask. A lightweight internal app where every account manager logs a weekly 60-second check-in (green / yellow / red, plus a sentence on what changed), and the leadership team sees a single portfolio dashboard. Yellow and red accounts surface. Stale accounts surface. Action items don't disappear into chat.

Why this case. It's universal — every services business, sales team, customer-success org, or partnerships group lives this exact problem. The data model is small enough to finish in 90 minutes. And the finished prototype is good enough to actually pilot on Monday.

Scope at a glance

  • 01Account list view with health colour
  • 02Account detail page · check-in history
  • 03New check-in form (status + notes + actions)
  • 04Portfolio dashboard for leadership
  • 05At-risk filter & stale-account flag

Personas in the room

  • AMAccount manager — logs check-ins weekly
  • PLPractice lead — scans portfolio Monday AM
  • MDManaging director — drills into red accounts

Out of scope (on purpose)

  • —Auth, billing, integrations, mobile native
  • —Real database (use mock data in v0)

01
00:00 — 00:15

Orient

Goal — shift the room from "AI is interesting" to "AI is my next hire."

Open by showing the finished ClientPulse prototype on screen. Click through it. Then say the words that change the day: we will rebuild this, from zero, together, in the next 105 minutes. Briefly define vibecoding (intent, not syntax). Set the ground rule: nobody opens a code editor.

Instructor checklist
  1. Demo the finished app live (90 sec)
  2. Define vibecoding in one sentence
  3. Show ChatPRD and v0 home screens side-by-side
  4. Confirm everyone has both tabs open and signed in
02
00:15 — 00:30

Frame the problem

Goal — produce a one-paragraph brief sharp enough to feed an AI.

Hand out the ClientPulse scenario card. Have each participant rewrite the problem in their own words, name the primary user, and write a single "if this app exists, what changes on Monday morning?" sentence. This step is the entire game — vibecoding fails when the brief is fuzzy.

Participant deliverable
  1. Problem in 3 sentences
  2. Primary user (one person, named)
  3. Success line — what changes when this exists
  4. Three features that MUST be in v1; one that won't
03
00:30 — 01:05

PRD with ChatPRD

Goal — turn the brief into a structured PRD that v0 can consume.

Open ChatPRD. Paste the seed prompt (see prompt library below). ChatPRD will ask clarifying questions — answer them in plain business language. The output is a real PRD with user stories, screens, and acceptance criteria. Resist the urge to edit too much; iterate by talking to it, not by rewriting Word docs.

Sequence
  1. Paste the seed prompt (see Prompt 01)
  2. Answer ChatPRD's clarifying questions in your own voice
  3. Ask it to add user stories per screen
  4. Ask it to write acceptance criteria for the at-risk filter
  5. Export the PRD — keep the tab open, you'll quote from it
04
01:05 — 01:45

Prototype with v0

Goal — produce a clickable, deployed prototype of all four screens.

Open Vercel v0. Paste the screen-by-screen prompt (see Prompt 02). v0 will generate a full UI in seconds. Now the real skill: iterating in plain English. "Make the red accounts more urgent." "The dashboard feels heavy — try a quieter layout." "Add a column for days since last check-in." Each turn produces a new version. Pick favorites. Deploy.

Build order (do not skip)
  1. Generate the portfolio dashboard first — the executive view
  2. Then the account list with health filters
  3. Then the account detail page
  4. Then the new check-in form
  5. Hit "Deploy" — get a shareable URL
05
01:45 — 02:00

Showcase & reflect

Goal — three participants demo; everyone leaves with a clear next action.

Three volunteers screenshare their deployed prototype for 90 seconds each. Not a beauty contest — focus the debrief on the gap between their original brief and the final result. Where did the AI misread intent? Where did they course-correct well? Close by asking every participant to name one problem on their desk this week they'll prototype themselves.

Close-out
  1. 3 volunteer demos · 90 seconds each
  2. Group reflection — what surprised you?
  3. Each person commits to one problem they'll vibecode this week
  4. Share the prompt library & deployed URLs in the chat
/ 04

The prompt library.

Prompt 01 · ChatPRD

The seed prompt

Paste this first. Then converse.
I'm a non-technical executive. I want a PRD for an internal web app called ClientPulse. Context: A 40-person consulting firm with 32 active client accounts. Account health lives in spreadsheets and Slack. Renewals get ambushed by problems leaders should have seen coming. Users: account managers (log weekly check-ins), practice leads (scan portfolio), managing director (drills into red accounts). Core screens I want covered: 1. Portfolio dashboard (leadership view, at-risk surfaced) 2. Account list (filter by health, sort by last check-in) 3. Account detail page (history of check-ins) 4. New check-in form (green/yellow/red, notes, action items) Out of scope: auth, billing, integrations, mobile native, real database. Please ask me 3–5 clarifying questions before writing the PRD. Keep the PRD tight — user stories, screens, acceptance criteria. No fluff.
Prompt 02 · Vercel v0

The build prompt

Start with the dashboard. Iterate per screen.
Build the portfolio dashboard for an internal B2B account-health app called ClientPulse. Top of page: 4 stat tiles — total accounts (32), green (21), yellow (8), red (3). Clean, restrained, no gradients. Below: a table of accounts. Columns: account name, owner (avatar + name), ARR, health (colored dot), last check-in (relative time, red if > 14 days), trend arrow. Sidebar: filters for health, owner, days-since-check-in. Top-right: "Log check-in" button (primary action). Use mock data — 12 realistic-looking B2B account names. Use Tailwind. Keep typography tight. Avoid heavy shadows. Treat red accounts with quiet urgency, not screaming red. After you generate this, I'll ask for the next screen.
Prompt 03 · Vercel v0

Iteration moves

Use these one at a time. Read the result. Pick or reject.
→ "Make red accounts feel urgent without being loud." → "The dashboard feels busy — quieter layout, more whitespace." → "Add a column showing days since last check-in. Red over 14." → "Replace the avatar circles with initials in subtle squares." → "Make this feel like a tool for senior partners, not a SaaS startup." → "Show what happens when an account manager clicks an account — generate that page next." → "Add a sticky filter bar so I can scan 200 accounts."
Prompt 04 · ChatPRD

Sharpening prompts

After the first PRD draft. Use to deepen specific sections.
→ "Write user stories from the practice lead's POV for the portfolio dashboard." → "Write acceptance criteria for the at-risk filter — what counts as at-risk?" → "What's the smallest possible v1 we could ship in two weeks?" → "What edge cases am I missing? Be blunt." → "Rewrite the PRD assuming the reader is a designer who's never used the product."

What goes wrong — and how to recover

  • DriftThe AI builds a beautiful screen that solves a different problem. Recover by pasting one line from your original brief and asking what it ignored.
  • BloatYou let v0 add features mid-flow. Hold the line — out-of-scope today is out-of-scope today. Iterate on what exists.
  • Aesthetic spiralYou spend 20 minutes on font choices. Set a 5-minute cap per visual iteration. Move on.
  • Vague promptOutput feels generic. Always answer: who sees this, what are they doing, what changes after they use it.
  • No commitYou don't deploy. The exercise only sticks if a real URL exists. Deploy before the showcase.
